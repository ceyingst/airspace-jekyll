  • Loving Literature: A Cultural History

    To do justice to the affective dimensions of the history of literariness, this book traces the institutions, reading practices, and etiquettes of appreciation that were developed to bring poetry and fiction home, into actual interiors and the interior spaces of the mind. It considers treatises on taste, early literary histories, the new science of bibliography, testaments of bibliophilia, almanacs, commonplace books, albums, physicians’ neurological accounts of reading, and middlebrow travelogues mapping authors’ homes and haunts.
